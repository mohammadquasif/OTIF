# Digital Transformation in Indian SMEs

## Abstract
This research investigates digital transformation impact on SME performance in India using a mixed-methods approach with survey data from 200 SMEs.

## Introduction
Digital transformation is critical for business competitiveness in the 21st century. SMEs form the backbone of the Indian economy, contributing approximately 30% to GDP and employing over 110 million people.

## Literature Review
Vial (2019) provides a comprehensive framework for understanding digital transformation. Resource-based view theory suggests firms achieve competitive advantage through unique resources and capabilities. The Digital India initiative launched in 2015 created a policy environment conducive to technology adoption.

## Methodology
This study employs a mixed-methods design with quantitative surveys of 200 SME owners across Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, and Gujarat. Thematic analysis follows Braun and Clarke (2006).

## Results
The survey achieved a 78% response rate. SMEs with higher digital maturity scores reported 23% higher revenue growth. Mobile technology adoption emerged as the most impactful digital initiative. However, 45% identified inadequate digital skills as a primary barrier.

## Discussion
The findings corroborate Vial (2019) while extending to the SME context in emerging economies. Digital skills gaps have important policy implications.

## Conclusion
Digital transformation positively impacts SME performance in India. Benefits are contingent on overcoming digital literacy and infrastructure barriers.
