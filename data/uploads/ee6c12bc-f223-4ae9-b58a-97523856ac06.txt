# Digital Transformation in Indian SMEs

## Abstract
This research investigates digital transformation impact on SME performance in India using a mixed-methods approach.

## Introduction
Digital transformation is critical for business competitiveness. SMEs form the backbone of the Indian economy, contributing approximately 30% to GDP and employing over 110 million people.

## Literature Review
Vial (2019) provides a comprehensive framework for understanding digital transformation. Resource-based view theory suggests firms achieve competitive advantage through unique resources.

## Methodology
This study employs a mixed-methods design with surveys of 200 SME owners across Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, and Gujarat. Thematic analysis follows Braun and Clarke (2006).

## Results
The survey achieved a 78% response rate. SMEs with higher digital maturity scores reported 23% higher revenue growth. However, 45% identified inadequate digital skills as a barrier.

## Discussion
The findings corroborate Vial (2019) while extending to the SME context in emerging economies.

## Conclusion
Digital transformation positively impacts SME performance in India, though benefits are contingent on overcoming digital literacy and infrastructure barriers.
